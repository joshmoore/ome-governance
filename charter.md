# **OME Governance Charter**

## **1. Purpose and Scope**

The Open Microscopy Environment (OME) develops and maintains open-source
software, specifications, and community infrastructure that support
interoperable biological imaging workflows and data management. OME provides
shared technologies, standards, and coordination mechanisms intended to support
sustainable and reusable scientific infrastructure across the bioimaging
ecosystem.

This charter defines a lightweight governance framework intended to support the
long-term sustainability, continuity, transparency, and scalability of OME. The
framework defines responsibilities, decision-making pathways, and coordination
structures that enable contributors and projects to operate effectively within
a shared ecosystem.

OME governance is intended to support operational continuity, responsible
stewardship, and constructive collaboration. Authority and responsibility are
delegated to individuals and groups with demonstrated and sustained operational
involvement in the work of the community. Governance exists to support
contribution, maintain continuity, and ensure that critical infrastructure and
standards remain stable, accessible, and community-driven.

This framework is intended to evolve incrementally over time as projects,
responsibilities, and community needs change.

## **2. Guiding Principles**

OME governance is guided by the following principles:

- Governance should remain lightweight and operationally focused, minimising
  unnecessary bureaucracy while preserving accountability and continuity.
- Responsibility follows sustained contribution and active engagement.
- Decision-making should be transparent and documented whenever practical.
- Authority should be delegated to the groups closest to the relevant technical
  or organizational work.
- OME should remain open to new contributors, projects, and collaborations.
  Growth should strengthen rather than dilute the integrity, sustainability,
  and shared identity of the OME ecosystem.
- Processes and structures should evolve incrementally in response to
  operational experience and community needs.

## **3. Governance Structure**

OME governance is organized as a layered structure that separates
ecosystem-level coordination from project-level operational governance.

### **3.1 OME Participation Agreement**

The Agreement defines the shared expectations and participation framework
across the OME ecosystem. It establishes baseline principles for collaboration,
contribution, stewardship, and community participation across all governance
bodies and registered projects. Decision-makers in all governance structures
are required to publicly acknowledge the agreement before contributing.

### **3.2 OME Management Group (OMG)**

The OME Management Group (OMG) is responsible for the strategic coordination,
continuity, and operational oversight of the OME ecosystem as a whole. This
includes facilitating funding, partnership, and strategic opportunities,
encouraging OME projects to present a coherent voice where appropriate, and
fostering a culture of mutual support and collaboration among OME projects for
the benefit of the whole project.

Further, the OMG:

- Maintains the overall governance framework.
- Oversees the recognition and review of OME Registered Projects.
- Coordinates responsibilities not delegated to specific projects.
- Provides escalation and conflict-resolution pathways where necessary.
- Supports continuity of shared infrastructure, standards, and community processes.
- Maintains ecosystem-level governance records and rosters.

The OMG delegates operational authority wherever practical and is not intended
to centralize all technical or project-level decision-making within OME.

### **3.3 OME Registered Projects (ORPs)**

OME Registered Projects (ORPs) are recognized projects operating within the OME
ecosystem. ORPs are responsible for advancing clearly defined technical,
organizational, or community goals aligned with the mission of OME.

Each ORP:

- Maintains a defined scope and governance structure, which may define
  additional internal governance layers where appropriate.
- Operates day-to-day according to its own charter and processes.
- Provides clear onboarding and participation pathways for contributors.
- Coordinates with the OMG on high-level governance changes and ecosystem-wide concerns.
- Maintains public governance and participation information wherever practical.

ORPs may manage multiple code repositories, specifications, services, datasets,
websites, or related resources. These resources are not required to reside
within a single organization or infrastructure platform.

The OMG may approve new ORPs based on demonstrated activity, sustainability,
governance readiness, and relevance to the broader OME mission.

## **4. Operational Coordination**

The OMG coordinates the operation of the OME ecosystem through regular
communication with OME Registered Projects and other governance bodies.

This coordination includes:

- Maintaining shared governance documents and public rosters.
- Tracking delegated responsibilities across projects and governance bodies.
- Providing escalation pathways where cross-project coordination or conflict
  resolution is required.
- Supporting continuity of critical infrastructure, standards, code
  repositories, services, and community resources.
- Encouraging collaboration and information sharing between ORPs.
- Coordinating ecosystem-wide meetings, announcements, and governance updates.

Governance authority is delegated together with responsibility for timely
execution, public communication, and operational continuity.

OME governance bodies are expected to operate transparently wherever practical.
Governance decisions, membership changes, major procedural updates, and
significant structural changes should be documented publicly.

ORPs are expected to maintain:

- Public communication channels.
- Clear onboarding pathways for contributors.
- Publicly accessible governance documentation.
- Timely responsiveness to community questions and coordination requests.

The OMG may periodically review governance structures, delegated
responsibilities, and project activity to ensure that responsibilities remain
sustainable and appropriately maintained.

## **5. Governance Documents**

OME governance is described through a set of related documents that define
responsibilities, membership, authority, and operational procedures at
different levels of the ecosystem.

These documents include:

- The OMG Charter (this document)
- [The OME Participation Agreement](agreements/participation-agreement.md)
- [The OMG Roster](omg/roster.md)
- [The Project Roster](projects.md)
- [ORP Charters and Rosters](./orp)

OME governance is organized as a layered structure:

- The Participation Agreement defines ecosystem-wide participation expectations.
- The OMG Charter and OMG Roster define ecosystem-level coordination and oversight.
- The Project Roster defines recognized OME Registered Projects.
- ORP Charters and Rosters define the governance and participation structures of individual projects.
- Project-specific governance documents, e.g. Policies, may define additional
  operational or technical governance where necessary.

This layered approach allows ecosystem-level governance structures to remain
stable while enabling projects and operational groups to evolve their
procedures independently as needed.

## **6. Decision-Making**

OME governance bodies should prioritize consensus-based decision-making
wherever practical. When consensus cannot be reached within a reasonable
timeframe, formal voting mechanisms may be used.

Each governance body shall define:

- Quorum requirements
- Voting procedures
- Escalation pathways
- Membership expectations
- Documentation practices

Operational decisions should be made by the most appropriate delegated group
whenever possible. Escalation to higher governance levels should occur only
when necessary to resolve conflicts, procedural failures, or questions of
ecosystem-wide impact.

Governance bodies are expected to make decisions within reasonable timeframes
and avoid unnecessary procedural blocking.

## **7. Participation and Membership**

Participation within OME is open to contributors who engage constructively with
the community and its projects.

Governance responsibilities are earned through sustained and substantive
contribution, operational involvement, and demonstrated commitment to the
long-term health of the ecosystem.

OME governance is intended to support active contributors rather than function
as a broad representative structure. Contributors are encouraged to participate
through projects, working groups, specifications, software maintenance,
documentation, outreach, infrastructure support, and related activities.

Projects and governance bodies should maintain clear and accessible pathways
for contributors to increase their responsibilities over time.

## **8. Creation and Evolution of ORPs**

The OMG may recognize new OME Registered Projects based on:

- A clear and relevant mission
- Demonstrated activity and sustainability
- Defined governance structures
- Active contributors or maintainers
- Alignment with OME objectives and values

ORPs are expected to maintain public documentation describing:

- Their scope
- Governance structure
- Membership
- Communication channels
- Participation pathways

The OMG may periodically review ORPs to ensure continued activity,
sustainability, and alignment with the broader OME ecosystem.

The OMG may also restructure, merge, sunset, or reassign responsibilities
between ORPs where necessary to maintain operational continuity and ecosystem
sustainability.

## **9. Community Coordination**

OME maintains a set of shared coordination mechanisms intended to support
communication and collaboration across projects and governance bodies.

These may include:

- Recurring community meetings
- Annual or periodic community events
- Shared communication channels
- Public governance discussions
- Shared infrastructure and services
- Cross-project technical coordination activities

Representatives of governance bodies and ORPs are encouraged to participate
regularly in ecosystem-wide coordination activities.

## **10. Transparency and Records**

OME governance documents, project rosters, charters, policies, and major
governance decisions should be publicly accessible wherever practical.

Governance bodies are encouraged to:

- Publish meeting summaries or decision logs.
- Maintain up-to-date membership rosters.
- Clearly document delegated responsibilities and escalation pathways.
- Record significant governance changes publicly.
- Maintain publicly accessible governance and participation documentation.

Transparency is intended to support continuity, accountability, operational
clarity, and community participation without introducing unnecessary procedural
overhead.

## **11. Amendments and Evolution**

This governance framework is expected to evolve over time in response to
operational experience and community growth.

The OMG is responsible for maintaining and updating this charter. Subordinate
governance documents, including ORP Charters and operational policy documents,
may define additional procedures consistent with this framework.

Changes to ecosystem-level governance structures should be documented publicly
and reviewed through transparent community processes wherever practical.

The OMG may delegate responsibility for maintaining subordinate governance
documents to ORPs or other governance bodies where appropriate.
